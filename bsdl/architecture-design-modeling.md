# 3. Architecture, Design & Modeling

---

> **Architecture, Design & Modeling define how software systems are conceived, structured, communicated, and evolved before and throughout implementation.**
> 

This section establishes the engineering disciplines responsible for transforming problems into coherent software solutions.

It provides the concepts, methods, artifacts, and decision-making processes required to design systems that are understandable, maintainable, scalable, and adaptable over time.

Within BSDL, architecture and design are continuous engineering activities rather than isolated project phases.

---

# Purpose

Architecture, Design & Modeling exist to provide engineers with a systematic approach for transforming ideas, requirements, and constraints into coherent software systems.

Rather than focusing solely on implementation, this discipline emphasizes understanding the problem domain, evaluating trade-offs, documenting decisions, and creating structures that support long-term evolution.

---

# Objectives

- Transform requirements into software structures.
- Model systems before implementation.
- Improve communication among stakeholders.
- Reduce architectural complexity.
- Support informed engineering decisions.
- Promote maintainability and sustainability.
- Establish reusable architectural knowledge.
- Enable AI-assisted reasoning through explicit models.

---

# Principles

- Design is continuous.
- Architecture communicates intent.
- Every architectural decision has a rationale.
- Models simplify complexity.
- Simplicity is preferable to cleverness.
- Architecture evolves with knowledge.
- Every abstraction has a purpose.
- Structure should emerge from the problem domain.
