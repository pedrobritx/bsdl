# Preamble

BSDL exists to define a software development philosophy that begins before process and reaches deeper than methodology. It is not a lifecycle recipe, a tooling guide, or a management framework. It is a constitution for how software should be conceived, justified, built, maintained, repaired, evolved, and retired.

Software is treated here as a socio-technical artifact: invented, collective, contextual, ethical, and historically situated. It is never neutral. It affects knowledge, labor, institutions, bodies, infrastructure, and material conditions. The framework therefore must address not only engineering efficiency, but also justice, accessibility, sustainability, and human flourishing.

## Why this constitution exists

1. To prevent software from being reduced to fashionable process theater.
2. To derive engineering practice from explicit philosophy instead of habit.
3. To make context a first-class input to every major decision.
4. To preserve reasoning, history, and accountability in durable form.
5. To ensure that human wellbeing outranks technical vanity.

## Core stance

BSDL begins with the assumption that software is built by people inside society, from shared knowledge, for human purposes. Therefore, every technical choice has ethical weight. Every architecture is political in its consequences. Every lifecycle is a trade-off. Every optimization has a cost.

## Scope boundary

BSDL does not attempt to dictate every tactical coding style. It tries to determine what kinds of tactical styles are acceptable, when they should be preferred, and which ones are incompatible with the constitution.
